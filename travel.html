TRAVEL

Buses

Buses in Mallorca are an efficient and affordable way to explore the island, offering a reliable network that connects major towns, cities, and tourist hotspots. Operated primarily by TIB (Transport de les Illes Balears) and EMT in Palma, these services are modern, air-conditioned, and generally punctual. Whether you're heading to Palma’s historic center, the beaches of Alcúdia, or the scenic mountain villages of Sóller and Valldemossa, buses provide an eco-friendly option. Tickets can be purchased on board or via apps, with discounts available for contactless payments. However, during peak tourist seasons, buses can become crowded, so it's a good idea to plan ahead to secure your seat.

TIB

TIB (Transport de les Illes Balears) is Mallorca's primary public transport network, offering bus, train, and metro services across the island. Known for its bright red and yellow buses, TIB connects Palma with coastal resorts, mountain villages, and rural towns, making it easy for both locals and tourists to travel sustainably. The network is modern, well-maintained, and supports cashless payments for added convenience.

EMT

EMT (Empresa Municipal de Transports) is Palma's local public transport service, operating an extensive network of blue buses within the city and its surrounding areas. EMT buses are frequent, affordable, and cover major attractions like Palma Cathedral, Bellver Castle, and nearby beaches such as Playa de Palma. With real-time tracking and contactless payment options, EMT offers a convenient way to navigate Palma without the need for a car.

Trains

Trains in Mallorca offer a scenic and relaxing way to explore the island, particularly its charming rural areas and historic towns. The island's rail network is operated by the Ferrocarril de Sóller, which runs a popular vintage wooden train between Palma and the picturesque town of Sóller. This iconic journey takes passengers through stunning landscapes, including the Tramuntana mountain range and olive groves. The train is a favorite for tourists, providing a unique experience while connecting Palma to Sóller in about an hour. In addition to the historic line, the island also offers modern train services operated by TIB, which connect Palma with other towns such as Manacor. Trains are a comfortable and scenic alternative to driving, with options for both single and return tickets. Whether you're seeking history, nature, or just a relaxing ride, Mallorca’s trains offer a pleasant and eco-friendly way to discover the island.

SFM

SFM is the largest train operator on the island, managing the primary railway network that connects Palma to major towns like Inca, Alaró, and other regions in central and western Mallorca. It operates modern, air-conditioned trains that provide a reliable and convenient way for both locals and tourists to travel across the island. SFM also oversees the Tren de Sóller, a historic railway that is one of Mallorca’s most famous attractions, linking Palma to the town of Sóller through scenic mountain landscapes.

Ferrocarril de Sóller

Although smaller in scale compared to SFM, the Ferrocarril de Sóller is one of the most iconic and significant train services in Mallorca. It operates the famous vintage wooden train between Palma and Sóller, a popular tourist route known for its stunning views of the Tramuntana mountains. The company also runs a tram service between Sóller and its port, connecting the town to the coast. The Ferrocarril de Sóller is particularly important as a tourist attraction, making it one of the key railway services in the region.

Taxis

Taxis are a convenient and popular way to get around Mallorca, offering a comfortable and direct mode of transport for both locals and visitors. Available throughout the island, taxis can be hailed on the street, found at designated taxi ranks, or booked in advance via phone or apps. They are regulated by the government, ensuring fair pricing and safety for passengers. Taxis are typically well-maintained, air-conditioned, and can accommodate up to four passengers. For longer trips or airport transfers, taxis provide a hassle-free option, offering door-to-door service. Though slightly more expensive than public transport, taxis are ideal for those who prefer flexibility, privacy, or need to travel to destinations not easily accessible by bus or train. Whether you're heading to the airport, a remote beach, or a mountain village, taxis in Mallorca provide a reliable and efficient way to explore the island.

  • Hailing a Taxi on the Street

    Taxis can be easily hailed directly from the street in most towns and cities. Look for taxis with a "TAXI" sign on the roof, which are available for hire. During busy times, such as in popular tourist areas or city centers, you may need to wait for a few minutes to catch one.

  • Taxi Ranks

    Taxi ranks (or "paradas de taxi") are located in major areas such as airports, train stations, and near popular attractions. These ranks are marked with a sign showing a taxi symbol and are the most reliable spots to find an available taxi. In Palma, for example, there are multiple ranks near the city’s main squares and beaches.

  • Booking by Phone

    You can call a taxi company directly to arrange a pick-up. Many companies provide phone numbers, and a taxi will arrive at the location in a short time. Some of the main taxi companies in Mallorca include Radio Taxi Palma (tel: +34 971 401 414) and Taxi Manacor (tel: +34 971 55 09 55).
